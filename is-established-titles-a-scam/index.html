Is Established Titles a scam?

A few months ago i almost bought myself Lordship in Scotland for $50 USD and waiting saved my pocket!

About 6 or so months ago i was watching a YouTube video, the sponsor was a company called "Established Titles" offering a chance to become a Lord or Lady based on a historic Scottish land ownership custom, where landowners have been long referred to as "Lairds", the Scottish term for "Lord", with the female equivalent being "Lady". Not only do you get Lordship,

"Every Lordship or Ladyship title pack contributes to the preservation and protection of woodland areas in Scotland."

You get to contribute to the environment in the process.¬†

This sounded super awesome to me and i decided to get myself Lordship! For some reason i forgot about it as the video went on and eventually forgot all about it until a few weeks later the same sponsor showed up on another video. I was hyped again until i heard something weird in the copy read...

Red Flags

The copy mentioned that "You can officially change your title on your credit card and air plane tickets". Huh.. why just credit cards and tickets? Why not my passport or ID? So i followed the YouTuber's affiliate link to a an all too familiar sight, a count-down timer. A count down time itself is not inherently evil but more often than not with websites that deal with an exchange of money - especially ones you are unfamiliar with - a count down is meant to push you into sending your money without thinking it through. You see, the timer is put there to create artificial stakes that drive our caveman brains into a "better get this quick before i lose it" state. And in that state you are way less critical and more likely to comply. So when i saw the timer i backed off a little but ended up forgetting about it completely.¬†

A few weeks pass and another YouTuber promotes them again. This time it was a finance channel that i follow quite a lot, so decided to give it a second chance and there it was.. another red flag! The last time i was on the site they had a 70% off special running for fathers' day or something. This time around they had 75% discount for some obscure holiday. Hmmmm.... why were their products 70% off for over 7 weeks!? So i immediately went to The Wayback Machine and picked a bunch of random¬† dates to see what the website looked like:

Investigation

With those waving red flags i decided to take a deeper look into Established Titles to figure out if i was being overly critical of them.¬†

Souvenir plot of land

The first thing that caught my attention was a this phrase:

I had never heard of souvenir land¬†before so i went looking. According to the Land Registration etc.¬† (Scotland) Act 2012:

This says a souvenir plot is NOT a registered plot, which means whomever is gifted said plot does not own it, which means that they are not a land owner and hence are not entitled to a Lordship! BOOM! Right there! Established Titles is basically running an NFT scam in the physical world. They sell souvenirs and print a pretty PDF that you get to keep with a false sense of value.¬†

Who are they?

Now that it was clear this is a scam, i wondered if i can find who's running it. I ran into a weird thing as i was trying to figure out who is behind this scam. The about us section was slightly different from what i remembered. It now has a disclaimer

The very fact that this disclaimer was added means that someone has caught on to this scam and Established Titles is trying to cover their hind sides. William Wolfram started Deal Dash - a sketchy business, made a fortune and started a bunch of sketchy business part of which are Kamikoto and Established Titles. If your favourite YouTuber showcases their beautiful crest and letter of Lordship advertising Established Titles - RUN!!!
